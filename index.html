Music Listening Test

Instructions

Welcome! In this short listening test, you'll help us evaluate some edited music clips. Here’s how it works:

  • First, you'll see the "Source Music" and a "Source Prompt". The "Source Music" is the original music clip. The "Source Prompt" is simply a text description of that original music (e.g., "An upbeat rock song").
  • Next, you'll see a "Target Prompt". This is a description of how we want to change the music (e.g., "An epic soundtrack").
  • Your Task: You will then hear an "Edited Result", which is a computer's attempt to apply that change. Your task is to judge how well the edited music matches the target description while still preserving the musical quality (like the melody and rhythm) of the original song.

Please use headphones for the best experience. There are no right or wrong answers—we're just interested in your opinion. Thank you for your help!

Source Music & Prompts

Source Music:

Source Prompt:

A recording of an upbeat rock song.

Target Prompt:

A recording of an epic soundtrack.

Edited Result

Question 1 (Balance): How would you rate the balance between the adherence of this edited music to the target prompt and its preservation of temporal structure (e.g., melody and rhythm) and musicality (e.g., harmonic, melodic, and pitch elements) from the original music?

Question 2 (Relevance): How would you rate the semantic closeness between this edited music and the target prompt?

Question 3 (Consistency): How would you rate the consistency of pitch contour and structural aspects between the original and edited music?
